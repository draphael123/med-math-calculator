How to Use This Tool

A quick guide for clinical ops staff at Fountain Vitality

🎯
What this tool does
Medication math for HRT and TRT patients — no manual calculator needed

Whenever a patient reports running out early, has too much medication, or had a dose change mid-cycle, you need to verify their supply math. This tool does that math for you — just enter the values from Akute and it shows you exactly how much they should have left.

1
Pick a calculator
Use the sidebar to navigate. Choose the calculator that matches the patient's medication type.
2
Pull values from Akute
Go to Medications tab → "More Info". Note the fill date, volume dispensed, and prescribed dose.
3
Enter the values
Type in the numbers — results appear automatically as you type. No submit button needed.
4
Read the status
A colored box tells you if the patient's claim checks out. Green = on track. Amber = check further. Red = discrepancy.
Color codes
Green — on track
Amber — review
Red — discrepancy

💡
Important reminders
Read these before your first calculation
  • Always get the fill date from Akute Medications tab → "More Info" — not from what the patient says
  • Phase 1 HRT patients receive 2 dispensers (34.5 mL each = 69 mL total) — confirm before concluding they are out
  • TRT injection patients report doses in syringe units, not mg — always convert before comparing to the chart
  • Any dose change mid-cycle means you must do a two-segment calculation — the HRT Cream calculator has a built-in tab for this
  • When routing to R&C, always paste your calculation in the ticket — show your work
  • TRT cream is a controlled substance — early refill requires a valid clinical reason and provider review

Overview

Universal supply calculator — works for any medication

🏁
When does math matter?
Three situations require supply calculations
📦 Early Refill Request
Patient says they are running out sooner than expected
📬 Oversupply Complaint
Patient has too much and wants to pause or delay
📝 Dose Change
Increase or decrease changes how long supply lasts
⚠️
The goal is always: given what was dispensed and what the patient should take, how long should this supply last — and does their report match?
📐
Core Formula
Every calculation uses this same logic
Days of Supply
Total Volume Dispensed ÷ Daily Usage = Days of Supply
Expected Remaining
Days of Supply − Days Since Dispensed = Days Remaining

Before doing any math, check:

  1. What does the prescription say? (Akute → Medications tab + most recent provider note)
  2. What is the patient actually doing? (Ask directly and/or request photos)

TRT Injections

Testosterone Cypionate • 200 mg/mL • converts mg → mL → syringe units

💉
Key Facts
Testosterone Cypionate • 200 mg/mL • 10 mL vial
Standard concentration: 200 mg/mL
Vial size: 10 mL = 2,000 mg total
biw = twice weekly (e.g., Mon + Thu)
Syringe units: 1 unit = 0.01 mL on a standard 1 mL syringe
Volume (mL) × 100 = syringe units
⚠️
If a patient draws to a unit mark not in their chart (e.g., "I draw to 45" but chart says 60 mg biw = 30 units), they may be overdosing. Document and notify provider immediately.
