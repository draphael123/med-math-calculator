How to Use This Tool

A quick guide for clinical ops staff at Fountain Vitality

🎯
What this tool does
Medication math for HRT and TRT patients — no manual calculator needed

Whenever a patient reports running out early, has too much medication, or had a dose change mid-cycle, you need to verify their supply math. This tool does that math for you — just enter the values from Akute and it shows you exactly how much they should have left.

1
Pick a calculator
Use the sidebar to navigate. Choose the calculator that matches the patient's medication type.
2
Pull values from Akute
Go to Medications tab → "More Info". Note the fill date, volume dispensed, and prescribed dose.
3
Enter the values
Type in the numbers — results appear automatically as you type. No submit button needed.
4
Read the status
A colored box tells you if the patient's claim checks out. Green = on track. Amber = check further. Red = discrepancy.
Color codes
Green — on track
Amber — review
Red — discrepancy

Important reminders
Read these before your first calculation
  • Always get the fill date from Akute Medications tab → "More Info" — not from what the patient says
  • Phase 1 HRT patients receive 2 dispensers (34.5 mL each = 69 mL total) — confirm before concluding they are out
  • TRT injection patients report doses in syringe units, not mg — always convert before comparing to the chart
  • Any dose change mid-cycle means you must do a two-segment calculation — the HRT Cream calculator has a built-in tab for this
  • When routing to R&C, always paste your calculation in the ticket — show your work
  • TRT cream is a controlled substance — early refill requires a valid clinical reason and provider review

Overview

Universal supply calculator — works for any medication

🏁
When does math matter?
Three situations require supply calculations
📦 Early Refill Request
Patient says they are running out sooner than expected
📬 Oversupply Complaint
Patient has too much and wants to pause or delay
📝 Dose Change
Increase or decrease changes how long supply lasts
⚠️
The goal is always: given what was dispensed and what the patient should take, how long should this supply last — and does their report match?
📐
Core Formula
Every calculation uses this same logic
Days of Supply
Total Volume Dispensed ÷ Daily Usage = Days of Supply
Expected Remaining
Days of Supply − Days Since Dispensed = Days Remaining

Before doing any math, check:

  1. What does the prescription say? (Akute → Medications tab + most recent provider note)
  2. What is the patient actually doing? (Ask directly and/or request photos)

Dose Change Guide

Any mid-cycle dose change requires a two-segment calculation

🔄
Dose Change — Recalculation Steps
Follow this process whenever the provider changes the dose between fills
1

Note the dose change date

Found in provider visit note or Akute chart note

2

Calculate usage at old dose

Fill date → dose change date × old daily usage = mL used

3

Subtract from total dispensed

Total dispensed − Segment 1 usage = remaining at change date

4

Calculate remaining days at new dose

Remaining ÷ new daily usage = days left after change

5

Find new run-out date

Dose change date + days remaining = new estimated run-out date

💡 Use HRT Cream → Mid-Cycle Dose Change tab for the automated version of steps 2–5.
SituationAction
Provider increases doseRecalculate immediately. Supply runs out sooner. Flag R&C with new estimated run-out date.
Provider decreases doseRecalculate. Patient may have more supply than expected. Educate on BUD.
Patient self-adjusts doseDocument in Akute. Notify provider. Calculate at both prescribed and self-reported dose.
Patient misses dosesMore supply than expected — educate on compliance.
Patient switches formNew calculation from switch date. Old supply math no longer applies.

Early Refill

Use every time a patient reports running low before expected refill date

⚠️
Early Refill Decision Framework
Six-step checklist before routing anything
1

Verify fill date and dispense amount

Akute → Medications tab. Note fill date, total volume/count, and prescribed dose.

2

Do the math

Calculate expected days of supply → days elapsed → how much should remain. Use the appropriate calculator in the sidebar.

3

Request photos or count

Ask for dispenser photo or remaining tablet count. Compare to your calculation.

4

Check for dose changes

Any dose increases since fill date? Recalculate with segments.

5

Check for compliance issues

Using more than prescribed? Self-adjusting? Document and notify provider.

6

Determine next step

See outcomes below.

OutcomeAction
Math checks out — Patient genuinely running lowRoute to R&C with calculation documented. Always show your work.
Math does not check outEducate on correct dosing before routing anywhere.
TRT Injection running out early — no refills remainRequires video visit. Cannot issue early refill without provider review.
📝
When routing to R&C for an early refill, always include your calculation in the ticket — show your work.

Tips & Common Pitfalls
  • Phase 1 patients receive 2 dispensers — always confirm before concluding they are out early
  • Check Akute Medications tab → "More Info" for days supply — not the IC sidebar (which can lag)
  • Use fill date from Akute or shipment tracking link in the IC right-hand sidebar
  • Dose prescribed is in mg for TRT injections — patients often report in syringe units; always convert
  • When routing to R&C, document your full calculation in the ticket
  • Self-adjusting testosterone dose: document and notify provider immediately
  • TRT injection early refill with no refills remaining → requires video visit, no exceptions
  • Dose change mid-cycle = two-segment calculation, not a simple average
